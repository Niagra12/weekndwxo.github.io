About The Weeknd

Abel Tesfaye, known as The Weeknd, is a Canadian artist known for his haunting vocals, emotional lyrics, and cinematic style. From underground mixtapes to Grammy wins, he's become one of the most influential musicians of his generation.

  • Full Name: Abel Makkonen Tesfaye
  • Date of Birth: February 16, 1990
  • Place of Birth: Toronto, Ontario, Canada
  • Nationality: Canadian
  • Ethnicity: Ethiopian descent
  • Stage Name: The Weeknd (dropped the "e" due to trademark issues with a Canadian band)

Career Highlights

  • 2010–2012: Gained recognition with mixtapes House of Balloons, Thursday, and Echoes of Silence. Later compiled into the 2012 release Trilogy.
  • 2013: Released debut studio album Kiss Land.
  • 2015: Achieved mainstream success with hits like “Earned It,” “The Hills,” and “Can’t Feel My Face” from the album Beauty Behind the Madness.
  • 2016: Released Grammy-winning album Starboy featuring Daft Punk.
  • 2020: Released After Hours, featuring the record-breaking hit “Blinding Lights.”
  • 2021: Headlined the Super Bowl LV Halftime Show.
  • 2022: Dropped concept album Dawn FM with an '80s-inspired style.
  • 2023: Starred in HBO’s The Idol.
  • 2025: Dropped the Hurry Up Tomorrow album and the movie With Jenna Ortega

Musical Style & Influences

The Weeknd blends R&B, pop, hip-hop, and electronic elements. His influences include Michael Jackson, Prince, R. Kelly, and David Bowie. His lyrics often explore themes of love, fame, loneliness, and excess.

Achievements

  • 4 Grammy Awards (though he boycotted them after a major snub in 2021)
  • Over 20 Juno Awards
  • Multiple Billboard Music Awards, including Top Artist
  • Guinness World Records holder, including:
    • Most monthly listeners on Spotify (surpassed 100 million)
    • First artist to reach 100 million monthly Spotify listeners

Other Notable Facts

  • Known for dark, cinematic visuals and concept albums
  • Actively supports causes such as racial justice, COVID-19 relief, and Ethiopian humanitarian aid
  • Has hinted at retiring The Weeknd persona and continuing under his birth name, signaling a new artistic direction
